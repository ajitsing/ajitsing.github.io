## <i class="fas fa-fire"></i> Top Stories This Week

### Tim Cook Steps Down as Apple CEO, John Ternus Takes Over - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2026/04/tim-cook-to-become-apple-executive-chairman-john-ternus-to-become-apple-ceo/){:target="_blank"}

On April 20, Apple [announced that Tim Cook is stepping down](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/20/tim-cook-stepping-down-as-apple-ceo-john-ternus-taking-over/){:target="_blank"} after 15 years as CEO. John Ternus, currently Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering, becomes the next CEO on September 1, 2026.

**The transition:**

Cook stays in the CEO seat through the summer to hand off and then moves to executive chairman, with a remit focused on engaging with policymakers around the world. Arthur Levinson, who has been non executive chairman for 15 years, becomes lead independent director on the same date. Johny Srouji is promoted to Chief Hardware Officer and takes over the Hardware Engineering group Ternus is leaving.

**Who Ternus is:**

Ternus joined Apple in 2001 and has [almost 25 years at the company](https://9to5mac.com/2026/04/20/apple-ceo-tim-cook-stepping-down-john-ternus-confirmed-as-new-apple-ceo/){:target="_blank"}. He has shipped iPad, AirPods, iPhone, Mac, and Apple Watch hardware. The board approved the move unanimously after a long succession process. The Verge called it [the end of an era](https://www.theverge.com/tech/915213/tim-cook-apple-ceo-stepping-down-john-ternus){:target="_blank"} that started when Cook took over from Steve Jobs in 2011.

**Why developers should care:**

This is a hardware engineer running a software heavy era. Ternus' Apple is going to ship under different pressures than Cook's. Apple Intelligence, on device AI, the M chip roadmap, and Vision Pro are all his calls now. Expect harder hardware bets and more vertical integration. The first real signal will be WWDC in June, which is now Cook's last as CEO and Ternus' first big appearance as the named successor.

### OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 for Agentic Coding - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/){:target="_blank"}

On April 23, OpenAI [shipped GPT-5.5](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/23/openai-releases-gpt-5-5-a-fully-retrained-agentic-model-that-scores-82-7-on-terminal-bench-2-0-and-84-9-on-gdpval/){:target="_blank"}, a fully retrained model built around long horizon agentic work. The rollout went to ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise plans plus Codex. API access follows once the extra cybersecurity safeguards land.

**The benchmark numbers:**

GPT-5.5 lands at 58.6 percent on SWE-Bench Pro, 82.7 percent on Terminal-Bench 2.0, and [84.9 percent on GDPval](https://www.siliconreport.com/openai-releases-gpt-5-5-targets-agentic-coding-and-enterprise-workflows-fd43b4218588ee5a){:target="_blank"}. That is a clear gain over GPT-5.4 on agent and computer use tasks but stays below Claude Opus 4.7's 64.3 percent on SWE-Bench Pro from last week. So the leaderboard sits split. Opus 4.7 still wins single shot coding. GPT-5.5 wins terminal work and the GDPval knowledge bundle.

**What changed under the hood:**

OpenAI says the model has fewer hand off points where the user has to nudge it back on track, better understanding of system architecture, and tighter reasoning about failures. Per token latency is similar to GPT-5.4 but the model finishes tasks with fewer tokens, so the bill should be lower per equivalent task.

**Where it lives:**

GPT-5.5 also showed up the same day in Codex as the new default model, with browser use for local dev servers and an automatic approval reviewer agent. The Codex CLI 0.124.0 release added reasoning controls, multi environment session management, Amazon Bedrock support, and a remote plugin marketplace. CLI 0.125.0 followed on April 24 with Unix socket transport and remote plugin installation.

### Anthropic Commits $100B to AWS for 5 Gigawatts - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-amazon-compute){:target="_blank"}

On April 20, Anthropic and Amazon announced an expansion to one of the largest cloud deals in industry history. Anthropic will [spend over 100 billion dollars on AWS](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/20/anthropic-takes-5b-from-amazon-and-pledges-100b-in-cloud-spending-in-return/){:target="_blank"} over the next ten years. Amazon is putting in 5 billion dollars now and up to 20 billion more tied to milestones, on top of its earlier 8 billion dollar investment.

**The compute number:**

The deal locks in [up to 5 gigawatts of capacity](https://abcnews.com/Business/wireStory/ai-startup-anthropic-commits-100-billion-amazons-aws-132244392){:target="_blank"} for training and serving Claude. The chips are Trainium2, Trainium3, and Trainium4, with options on future generations. Significant Trainium2 capacity is online in Q2 2026 and the goal is nearly 1 gigawatt of combined Trainium2 and Trainium3 capacity by end of year.

**Why it matters:**

Over 100,000 customers run Claude on Bedrock today. The two companies already operate Project Rainier, one of the largest AI clusters in the world. The new deal adds Asia and Europe inference expansion. For developers, this is the strongest signal yet that Bedrock is now a first class place to ship Claude based products. The Mythos preview from earlier this month is still gated through Glasswing but the underlying capacity buildout is no longer in doubt.

### Google Cloud Next 2026: Gemini 3.1 and the Enterprise Agent Platform - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/infrastructure-and-cloud/google-cloud/google-cloud-next-26-recap/){:target="_blank"}

It includes Agent Studio for low code creation, Agent Registry for catalogs, Agent Identity, and Agent Gateway for traffic control. **8th generation TPUs:**

In a [blog post on April 22](https://www.aol.com/articles/google-says-75-companys-code-131748723.html){:target="_blank"}, Sundar Pichai said 75 percent of new code at Google is now AI generated and reviewed by engineers, up from 50 percent six months ago. ### Vercel Discloses Context.ai Breach Exposing Customer Secrets - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/20/developer-tooling-provider-vercel-discloses-breach-exposed-users-data/){:target="_blank"}

On April 20, Vercel published a [security bulletin](https://vercel.com/kb/bulletin/vercel-april-2026-security-incident){:target="_blank"} for a breach that started with a third party AI tool called Context.ai. A Vercel employee had given Context.ai access to their Google Workspace account. An attacker who had earlier compromised Context.ai used the OAuth token to log in as the employee and pull customer environment variables that were not flagged as sensitive.

**The leak:**

Stolen data was [posted on BreachForums for 2 million dollars](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/20/vercel_context_ai_security_incident/){:target="_blank"} by ShinyHunters, including data on hundreds of employees and a number of API keys. Some of those keys touched GitHub repositories. Vercel maintains the GitHub repository for Node.js, so supply chain risk is not zero.

**The fallout:**

[Crypto teams scrambled](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/04/20/hack-at-vercel-sends-crypto-developers-scrambling-to-lock-down-api-keys){:target="_blank"} to rotate keys because many Web3 dashboards and wallet UIs run on Vercel. Solana exchange Orca rotated all its deployment credentials. Vercel hired Mandiant, told customers to rotate non sensitive environment variables, and confirmed Next.js, Turbopack, and its open source projects are clean.

The lesson is the one supply chain people have been making for two years now. A third party AI tool with full Google Drive scope is a key into your whole org. Audit which AI tools your team has connected and what scopes they hold.

## <i class="fas fa-code"></i> Developer Tools & Platforms

### Cursor 3.2 Adds Async Sub Agents and Multi Root Workspaces - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://cursor.com/changelog/04-24-26){:target="_blank"}

Cursor 3.2 [shipped on April 24](https://cursor.com/changelog/04-24-26){:target="_blank"} with three notable changes. The new `/multitask` command spawns async sub agents that handle parts of a request in parallel instead of queuing them, which is the same direction GPT-5.5 and Kimi K2.6 are pushing. Worktrees got an upgrade so you can run isolated tasks across branches in the background and promote any branch to the foreground in one click. The biggest workflow change is multi root workspaces. A single agent session can now target a reusable workspace made of multiple folders, so you can make cross repository changes spanning frontend, backend, and shared libraries without retargeting the agent each time.

### Kimi K2.6 Promoted to GA with 12 Hour Runs and 300 Agent Swarms - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://kimi-k2.org/blog/24-kimi-k2-6-release){:target="_blank"}

Moonshot AI flipped Kimi K2.6 from preview to general availability on April 21, eight days after the beta. The trillion parameter MoE backbone is unchanged with 32 billion active parameters and 384 experts. What is new is the execution layer. The context window is now [262,144 tokens with automatic compression](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/04/20/moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k2-6-with-long-horizon-coding-agent-swarm-scaling-to-300-sub-agents-and-4000-coordinated-steps/){:target="_blank"} so 12 hour runs do not collapse at hour nine. Native primitives spawn and coordinate up to 300 sub agents across 4,000 steps in a single swarm. Benchmarks land at 66.7 percent on Terminal-Bench 2.0 and 58.6 percent on SWE-Bench Pro. Vercel reported a 50 percent improvement on its Next.js benchmark and Factory.ai a 15 percent gain. K2.6 is live on Kimi.com, the Kimi App, the API, and the Kimi Code CLI, with weights on Hugging Face.

### OpenAI Ships Workspace Agents in ChatGPT - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://9to5mac.com/2026/04/22/openai-updates-chatgpt-with-codex-powered-workspace-agents-for-teams/){:target="_blank"}

On April 22, OpenAI introduced Workspace Agents in ChatGPT, powered by Codex. They replace Custom GPTs for teams. Workspace Agents are shared agents that handle long running workflows inside an org's permissions and run in the cloud so they can keep working without a chat window open. They show up in ChatGPT and in Slack. The launch went to Business, Enterprise, Edu, and Teachers plans as a research preview.

### Eclipse Foundation Launches Open VSX Managed Registry - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://newsroom.eclipse.org/news/announcements/eclipse-foundation-launches-open-vsx-managed-registry-0){:target="_blank"}

On April 21, the Eclipse Foundation launched a [managed version of Open VSX](https://thenewstack.io/open-vsx-managed-registry/){:target="_blank"} for VS Code extensions. The free community registry stays free for developers and open source projects. The managed tier ships a 99.95 percent uptime SLA, multi region infrastructure, 24/7 monitoring, identity based access controls, and usage dashboards. Open VSX serves over 300 million downloads per month with peak daily traffic above 200 million requests across 12,000 extensions and 8,000 publishers. Initial customers are AWS, Google, and Cursor. The launch is positioned as the open alternative to Microsoft's VS Code Marketplace for AI native IDEs that need production grade reliability.

### Atlassian and Google Cloud Tie Rovo to Gemini Enterprise - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.googlecloudpresscorner.com/2026-04-22-Atlassian-Expands-Partnership-with-Google-Cloud-to-Power-Agentic-AI-for-Teams-Worldwide){:target="_blank"}

Announced at Google Cloud Next on April 22, the expanded partnership puts Gemini 3 Flash behind select Rovo capabilities and lets Rovo run inside Gemini Enterprise. Atlassian also committed to running its AI training workloads on Google Cloud. For teams already using Jira, Confluence, or Bitbucket with Rovo, this is the integration that lets the agent reach out to Workspace data through Gemini.

### OpenAI Opens Privacy Filter as an Open Weight Model - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-privacy-filter/){:target="_blank"}

On April 22, OpenAI released Privacy Filter, a 1.5 billion parameter open weight model with 50 million active parameters for detecting and masking personally identifiable information in text. The model supports up to 128,000 tokens of context and is built to run locally so you can do privacy preserving preprocessing without sending data to a hosted API. Useful in front of any logging, analytics, or fine tuning pipeline that touches user input.

### Ant Group Releases Ling-2.6-Flash - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260422256825/en/Ant-Group-Unveils-Ling-2.6-Flash-A-Major-Leap-in-AI-Efficiency){:target="_blank"}

Ant Group dropped Ling-2.6-Flash on April 22, a 104 billion parameter MoE model with only 7.4 billion parameters active. It is built around token efficiency, hits an Intelligence Index of 26 on roughly 15 million output tokens (which the company says is 86 percent fewer than comparable models), and runs at up to 340 tokens per second. Pricing is 10 cents per million input tokens and 30 cents per million output tokens. Worth a look if you want a cheap MoE for high throughput agent loops.

### GitHub Pauses Copilot Pro Sign-ups - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://github.blog/news-insights/company-news/changes-to-github-copilot-individual-plans/){:target="_blank"}

On April 20, GitHub stopped accepting new sign-ups for Copilot Pro, Pro+, and Student plans. The free plan still accepts new users. Joe Binder, VP of Product, [said agentic workflows](https://thenewstack.io/github-copilot-signups-paused/){:target="_blank"} have changed Copilot's compute profile and long running parallel sessions burn through far more capacity than the plan structure was built for. Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google have all adjusted limits in recent months.

## <i class="fas fa-shield-alt"></i> Security

### Microsoft Ships Emergency ASP.NET Core Patch for CVE-2026-40372 - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/dotnet/dotnet-10-0-7-oob-security-update/){:target="_blank"}

On April 21, Microsoft released an out of band patch for [CVE-2026-40372](https://github.com/dotnet/announcements/issues/395){:target="_blank"}, a critical privilege escalation flaw in ASP.NET Core. CVSS 9.1. The bug sits in `Microsoft.AspNetCore.DataProtection` versions 10.0.0 through 10.0.6 and was a regression introduced in .NET 10. The managed authenticated encryptor computed the HMAC over the wrong bytes of the payload and then discarded the hash. That lets an unauthenticated attacker forge protected payloads, bypass integrity validation, [forge authentication cookies](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-releases-emergency-security-updates-for-critical-aspnet-flaw/){:target="_blank"}, and gain SYSTEM access. The fix is .NET 10.0.7. Forged tokens stay valid until you rotate the DataProtection key ring, so patching alone is not enough.

### nginx UI CVE-2026-33032 Under Active Exploitation - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/etr-cve-2026-33032-nginx-ui-missing-mcp-authentication/){:target="_blank"}

[CVE-2026-33032](https://undercodenews.com/unauthenticated-nginx-ui-flaw-under-active-attack-cve-2026-33032-enables-full-server-takeover/){:target="_blank"} is a missing authentication bug on the `/mcp_message` endpoint of nginx UI. CVSS 9.8. The endpoint is part of the Model Context Protocol exposure and any unauthenticated attacker on default config can drive privileged MCP operations, change nginx config, restart services, redirect traffic, and drop backdoors. Active exploitation in the wild [was confirmed on April 22](https://www.prsol.cc/2026/04/22/critical-nginx-ui-auth-bypass-flaw-now-actively-exploited-in-the-wild/){:target="_blank"}. Internet scans show roughly 2,600 instances still exposed. The fixed version is 2.3.6. Get off anything 2.3.5 or below and lock down access to the management interface.

### CanisterWorm Hits Namastex Labs npm Packages - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/22/another_npm_supply_chain_attack/){:target="_blank"}

A self propagating npm worm hit packages owned by Namastex Labs starting April 21. Affected packages include `@automagik/genie`, `pgserve`, and `@fairwords/websocket`. The worm uses install time execution to steal tokens, credentials, API keys, SSH keys, and configurations from cloud services, CI/CD systems, registries, Kubernetes, Docker, and LLM platforms. It exfiltrates the data and then propagates to additional packages. The TTPs overlap with the earlier TeamPCP/CanisterWorm work. If you ship anything that touches Namastex packages, audit what ran on your CI machines this past week.

### GitHub Copilot Training Opt-Out Deadline April 24 - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://dev.to/devcodehub99/github-copilot-is-training-on-your-code-opt-out-before-april-24-or-lose-the-choice-mii){:target="_blank"}

GitHub updated its Copilot terms to expand what can be used for training including code context, accept and reject patterns, file content, and chat history from private repositories. The deadline to opt out under the previous terms was April 24. Past that date the opt out path is harder to reach. The setting lives at GitHub Settings, then Copilot, then Privacy. Toggle off "Allow GitHub to use my code snippets for product improvements" and "Allow GitHub to use my data for AI model training."

### Aikido Launches Endpoint Security for Developer Devices - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/20/3276846/0/en/Aikido-Security-Launches-Endpoint-Protection-for-Developer-Devices-as-Software-Supply-Chain-Attacks-Hit-Unprecedented-Scale.html){:target="_blank"}

On April 20, Aikido shipped Aikido Endpoint, a lightweight agent that inspects and blocks risky packages, IDE extensions, browser plugins, and AI tools before they install on developer machines. The pitch matches the threat model behind the Vercel breach the same day. Most supply chain attacks start with one developer running something they should not have on a workstation that has access to production credentials.

### Sundar Pichai Says 75% of New Code at Google Is AI Generated - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.aol.com/articles/google-says-75-companys-code-131748723.html){:target="_blank"}

In a [blog post on April 22](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/tech-news/google-ceo-sundar-pichai-says-ai-generates-75-codes-at-the-company-why-this-number-matters/articleshow/130451126.cms){:target="_blank"}, Sundar Pichai said 75 percent of new code at Google is AI generated and reviewed by engineers, up from around 50 percent in fall 2025 and 25 percent in October 2024. He framed it as "agentic" engineering, with engineers orchestrating autonomous workflows rather than prompting for snippets. One complex code migration was reportedly finished six times faster than would have been possible a year ago with engineers alone. The internal platform is called Antigravity. As with Snap last week, the data point lands directly into the AI as cause of layoffs narrative, even though Pichai did not connect the two.

### Anthropic Launches the Economic Index Survey - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-survey-announcement){:target="_blank"}

On April 22, Anthropic announced a monthly Economic Index Survey delivered through Anthropic Interviewer. It samples Claude users with accounts at least two weeks old, rotates the panel each month, and asks about tasks people are handing off to AI, productivity gains, hiring shifts, and expectations for the year ahead. The goal is to combine qualitative responses with usage data to detect economic changes earlier than labor market statistics can.

*Three threads worth pulling on. The first is that the major model labs are now shipping in lockstep. Opus 4.7 took back the SWE-Bench Pro lead on April 16. Kimi K2.6 hit GA on April 21 with 12 hour runs. GPT-5.5 landed on April 23 with the agentic and terminal numbers. Cursor 3.2 ships async sub agents on April 24. The shape of "what good agentic coding looks like" is now being defined by four products at once and the differences between them are getting small enough that your choice may end up being about pricing and which CLI you prefer rather than which model is best this week. The second is that Pichai's 75 percent number is going to do a lot of work. Last week's Snap memo cited 65 percent. Pichai's post cites 75. By next quarter every CEO writing a layoff memo will have a number in this range to point at. The number is honest in one sense and misleading in another. It says nothing about who is responsible for the code, how much was reviewed, or how much was rewritten. But it will be cited as if it does, and the cited percentage will keep climbing. The third is the Vercel breach. The path was AI tool with broad OAuth scope, employee Google Workspace takeover, then customer environment variables. That is not a Vercel problem. That is the shape of breaches in the agentic era. Every team that has wired Notion AI, Glean, Context.ai, or any other helper into their Workspace with full Drive scope has the same exposure. If you have not audited your OAuth grants in 2026, this is the week.*
