## <i class="fas fa-fire"></i> Top Stories This Week

### Anthropic Signs SpaceX for the Colossus 1 Supercluster - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.anthropic.com/news/higher-limits-spacex){:target="_blank"}

On May 6, Anthropic [announced a partnership with SpaceX](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/06/anthropic-spacex-data-center-capacity.html){:target="_blank"} to use all of the compute at SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis. The deal unlocks more than 300 megawatts of capacity and over 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs within the month.

**What changed for users on day one:**

Anthropic doubled Claude Code's five hour rate limits for Pro, Max, Team, and seat based Enterprise plans. The peak hours limit reduction for Pro and Max accounts is gone. API rate limits for Claude Opus models went up considerably. All three changes shipped the same day, not at some future date.

**The bigger picture:**

This sits next to Anthropic's other compute commitments: an [up to 5 GW agreement with Amazon](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-amazon-compute){:target="_blank"}, a 5 GW agreement with Google and Broadcom, a Microsoft and NVIDIA strategic partnership that includes 30 billion dollars of Azure capacity, and a 50 billion dollar US infrastructure investment with Fluidstack. Anthropic also said it has expressed interest in working with SpaceX on multiple gigawatts of orbital AI compute capacity.

**The Musk angle:**

Elon Musk had publicly called Anthropic "evil" not long ago. After the deal, [Musk said on X](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-06/anthropic-inks-computing-deal-with-spacex-to-meet-ai-demand){:target="_blank"} that he had spent time with the team and was satisfied they were focused on doing the right thing. The competitive picture inside the AI compute market keeps getting weirder.

### Cloudflare Cuts 1,100 in Agentic AI Pivot - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://www.cloudflare.com/press/press-releases/2026/cloudflare-announces-first-quarter-2026-financial-results/){:target="_blank"}

On May 7, Cloudflare reported Q1 2026 revenue of 639.8 million dollars, up 34 percent year over year and a beat against the 622 million dollar estimate. The same release announced a workforce reduction of approximately [1,100 employees](https://www.businessinsider.com/cloudflare-announces-1100-layoffs-amid-ai-focus-shift-2026-5){:target="_blank"}, around 20 percent of staff.

**The framing:**

{% include ads/in-article.html %}

CEO Matthew Prince called it a transition to an "agentic AI-first operating model." Internal AI usage at Cloudflare is up over 600 percent in three months. Prince said the company is keeping product builders and sellers but cutting the support roles sitting behind them. [TechCrunch reported](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/08/cloudflare-says-ai-made-1100-jobs-obsolete-even-as-revenue-hit-a-record-high/){:target="_blank"} that this is the first mass layoff in Cloudflare's 16 year history, with notifications hitting both work and personal email within an hour of the announcement.

**Numbers:**

Restructuring charges of 140 to 150 million dollars are expected, with 105 to 110 million in cash and 35 to 40 million in non cash equity costs, mostly hitting Q2 2026. Adjusted EPS came in at 25 cents versus the 23 cent estimate. Full year 2026 revenue is guided to 2.805 to 2.813 billion dollars. Cloudflare stock fell 24 percent on the day despite the earnings beat.

**Why developers should care:**

This is the largest dev-relevant layoff of the week and the clearest statement so far that an AI-native infrastructure vendor will cut headcount even when revenue is growing. Expect more of this framing from other infra companies through the rest of the year.

### AWS Ships Bedrock AgentCore Payments and the AWS MCP Server - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/whats-new/2026/04/amazon-bedrock-agentcore-payments-preview/){:target="_blank"}

On May 7, AWS [launched Amazon Bedrock AgentCore Payments in preview](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/07/amazon-rolls-out-ai-agent-stablecoin-payments-platform-with-coinbase-and-stripe){:target="_blank"} with Coinbase and Stripe. The service lets agents make stablecoin micropayments for APIs, MCP servers, web content, and other paid resources.

**How it works:**

When an agent hits an HTTP 402 from a paid resource, AgentCore handles x402 protocol negotiation, wallet authentication, the stablecoin payment, and proof delivery. The agent's reasoning loop never sees the interruption. Coinbase provides CDP wallet integration and the x402 Bazaar MCP server with more than 10,000 endpoints agents can discover and pay for. Stripe provides Privy wallet infrastructure. Session level spending limits are enforced at the infrastructure layer, and observability flows through standard AgentCore logs, metrics, and traces.

**Where it runs:**

US East (N. Virginia), US West (Oregon), Europe (Frankfurt), and Asia Pacific (Sydney). The current focus is micropayments for digital services. AWS said larger transactions like hotel bookings and travel reservations are on the roadmap.

**The MCP Server GA:**

The day before, on May 6, [AWS announced the AWS MCP Server is generally available](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/whats-new/2026/05/aws-mcp-server/){:target="_blank"} as the core component of the [Agent Toolkit for AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/whats-new/2026/05/agent-toolkit/){:target="_blank"}. Agents can now call any AWS API through a single tool, including operations that need file uploads or long running execution. Sandboxed Python script execution lets agents run multi step operations against AWS services without touching the local filesystem or shell. Agent Skills replace Agent SOPs with discoverable, on demand guidance to keep context windows lean. There is no per request charge, you pay only for the AWS resources the agents use.

### Cursor 3.3 Brings PR Review Into the Agents Window - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://cursor.com/changelog/05-07-26){:target="_blank"}

On May 7, Cursor 3.3 shipped with three headline features and a stack of smaller improvements.

{% include ads/display.html %}

**PR review:**

Take a PR from creation to merge inside Cursor. The Reviews tab shows inline review threads and top level PR comments. The Commits tab gives a focused view of the commit history. The Changes tab adds a file tree and a changes picker for moving around larger PRs. Reviewer status and pending review banners surface inline, and quick action pills handle the next step.

**Build in Parallel:**

Plans now run faster. Click Build in Parallel and Cursor identifies independent parts of the plan and runs them at the same time using async subagents. Dependent steps stay in order.

**Split PRs:**

A new quick action splits changes into multiple PRs. It uses chat context to find logical slices, defaults to independent PRs when dependencies allow, takes a backup snapshot before splitting, and proposes a split plan for approval.

**Smaller wins worth knowing:**

You can pin commonly used skills as quick action pills. Explore subagent behavior is now configurable in settings, including model choice and a kill switch. The slash multitask command is available in the editor for running async subagents in line. MCP connection behavior is more predictable with explicit stale token cleanup on re-auth.

### xAI Launches Grok 4.3 - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://venturebeat.com/ai/xai-launches-grok-4-3-at-an-aggressively-low-price-and-a-new-fast-powerful-voice-cloning-suite/){:target="_blank"}

On May 6, xAI released Grok 4.3 with built in reasoning as a permanent state, a 1 million token context window, and agentic tool use. Pricing is 1.25 dollars per million input tokens and 2.50 dollars per million output tokens.

**Same day extras:**

Connectors landed for SharePoint, Outlook, OneDrive, Google Workspace, Notion, GitHub, and Linear. The Grok Imagine Quality Mode API opened up. Custom voices and a voice library are available, and there is a new voice cloning suite.

**Migration deadline:**

xAI also published a [May 15, 2026 model retirement notice](https://docs.x.ai/developers/migration/may-15-retirement){:target="_blank"} that retires eight legacy models including grok-4-1-fast-reasoning, grok-4-fast-reasoning, grok-3, grok-code-fast-1, and grok-imagine-image-pro. Anyone calling these endpoints needs to migrate to Grok 4.3 by May 15 or expect failures.

### OpenAI Updates ChatGPT to GPT-5.5 Instant - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant/){:target="_blank"}

On May 5, OpenAI rolled out [GPT-5.5 Instant as the new default ChatGPT model](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-instant-system-card/){:target="_blank"} and as `chat-latest` in the API. The headline number is 52.5 percent fewer hallucinated claims than GPT-5.3 Instant on high stakes prompts covering medicine, law, and finance, plus 37.3 percent fewer inaccurate claims on flagged conversations. GPT-5.5 Instant is also tighter, with 30.2 percent fewer words and 29.2 percent fewer lines on the example shown in the launch post. The model is better at analyzing photo and image uploads, STEM questions, and deciding when to run web search. Personalization across past chats, connected files, and Gmail is rolling to Plus and Pro on the web first, then Free, Go, Business, and Enterprise. GPT-5.3 Instant stays available to paid users for three months under model configuration settings before retirement.

### OpenAI Ships Three New Realtime Audio Models - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://openai.com/index/advancing-voice-intelligence-with-new-models-in-the-api/){:target="_blank"}

On May 7, OpenAI introduced three new audio models in the API. GPT-Realtime-2 brings GPT-5 class reasoning to a voice model for handling complex requests and natural conversation. GPT-Realtime-Translate is a live translation model that accepts more than 70 input languages and produces 13 output languages. GPT-Realtime-Whisper is a streaming speech to text model that transcribes live as a user speaks. The set covers the three patterns OpenAI is pushing for voice: voice to action, systems to voice, and voice to voice.

### OpenAI Scales Trusted Access for Cyber and Trusted Contact - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-with-trusted-access-for-cyber/){:target="_blank"}

Also on May 7, OpenAI rolled out [GPT-5.5-Cyber in limited preview](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-5-with-trusted-access-for-cyber/){:target="_blank"} for cybersecurity defenders, behind verified access. Phishing resistant security is required by June 1. The same day, OpenAI [introduced Trusted Contact](https://openai.com/index/introducing-trusted-contact-in-chatgpt/){:target="_blank"}, an optional safety feature that lets adult ChatGPT users nominate an emergency contact for serious mental health and self harm concerns. The notification is intentionally limited and does not include chat transcripts. OpenAI also published [Testing ads in ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/testing-ads-in-chatgpt/){:target="_blank"} on May 7 and [New ways to buy ChatGPT ads](https://openai.com/index/new-ways-to-buy-chatgpt-ads/){:target="_blank"} on May 5, the first explicit ad surfaces inside ChatGPT itself.

### Google Cloud Ships Agent Identity GA and Agentic Data Cloud Updates - [<i class="fas fa-external-link-alt"></i>](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/identity-security/whats-new-in-iam-security-governance-and-runtime-defense){:target="_blank"}
